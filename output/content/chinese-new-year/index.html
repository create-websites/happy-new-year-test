Core Traditions & Symbolism

The festival brims with traditions to say goodbye to the old year and invite luck and success into the new one. This part highlights key customs, from festive foods to vibrant decorations.

🏮

Decorations

Homes are adorned with red lanterns, paper cuttings, and couplets bearing lucky phrases. The color red dominates, symbolizing joy, prosperity, and protection from evil.

🧧

Red Envelopes (Hongbao)

Married couples and elders give red envelopes containing money to children and unmarried adults. This tradition is not about the amount of money but about passing on good luck and blessings for the year ahead.

🧹

Cleaning & Prepping

The house is thoroughly cleaned before the New Year to clear out past bad luck. Cleaning tools are then stored to ensure good luck stays in the new year.

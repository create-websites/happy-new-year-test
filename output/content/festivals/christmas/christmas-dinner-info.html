A Global Christmas Plate

Though the spirit is shared, the menu is delightfully varied. This chart highlights favorite main dishes globally, revealing how culture and climate influence the table.

From Savory to Sweet: The Great Dessert Evolution

Many classic Christmas desserts began as savory meat dishes, but the drop in sugar prices after the 17th century turned them into the sweet treats we enjoy now.

14th Century: Plum Pottage

The forerunner of Christmas Pudding: a hearty boiled porridge made with beef broth, fruit, and spices, served as a meal starter.

13th-17th Century: Meat & Spice Pies

Early mince pies featured minced mutton and suet, shaped oblong to represent Jesus's manger, and served as a savory dish.

19th Century: The Sweet Transformation

Due to cheaper sugar, meat was dropped. Dickens' *A Christmas Carol* made sweet Christmas Pudding and Mince Pie iconic holiday treats.

The Reign of the Turkey

Brought to Europe from North America in the 1500s, the turkey rose to fame as a festive Christmas centerpiece, thanks to Queen Victoria and Charles Dickens.

Stuffing's Roman Roots

Stuffing dates back to 1st century AD Rome, where leftovers and herbs were used for flavor.

Brussels Sprouts: A Winter Staple

Originating near Brussels in the 13th century, this hearty winter crop turned into a Victorian Christmas favorite.

Cranberry Sauce: An American Original

In the 1940s, Ocean Spray's canning turned this native North American berry into a holiday staple.

The Modern, Inclusive Feast

Christmas dinner is changing. Families are embracing new traditions, swapping the classic turkey for meals that celebrate varied tastes, dietary preferences, and cultural roots.

A Birth in a Manger

The story recounts Mary and Joseph's journey from Nazareth to Bethlehem for a census. With no room at the inn, they found refuge in a stable, where Jesus was born and placed in a manger. Angels appeared to shepherds, announcing the birth, prompting them to visit and worship him. Soon after, three Magi from the East, led by a star, came to pay homage to the infant king, offering gifts of gold, frankincense, and myrrh. This humble birth is cherished as a powerful sign of God's love for the world.

What is Father's Day?

Father's Day celebrates fatherhood, paternal connections, and the impact of fathers on society. It's a time to show thanks and recognize the care, guidance, and support fathers and father figures offer.

The Origins of Father's Day

Father's Day, as we know it today, originated in the early 1900s in the U.S. It was inspired by Sonora Smart Dodd, who sought to celebrate her father.

üí°

A Daughter's Tribute

Sonora Smart Dodd was inspired by her father, William Jackson Smart, a Civil War veteran and single parent who raised six children alone. After a sermon on the new Mother's Day, she believed fathers deserved equal tribute. She advocated for Father's Day, leading to the first city-wide celebration in Washington on June 19, 1910. Over time, the idea spread, and in 1972, President Richard Nixon officially established it as a U.S. national holiday.

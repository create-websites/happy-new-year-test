From animated adventures to classic slashers, there’s something for every mood. Here’s a curated watchlist with a brief description of each pick and where to stream it.

1. Coraline (2009)

This stop‑motion fantasy follows a young girl who discovers a parallel world that’s both enchanting and sinister. Its eerie atmosphere and imaginative visuals make it a family favorite. Where to stream: Netflix or Amazon Prime Video.

2. Halloween (1978)

John Carpenter’s slasher classic introduces Michael Myers, an escaped murderer who terrorizes a suburban neighborhood on Halloween night. It’s essential viewing for horror fans. Where to stream: Shudder or AMC+.

3. Hocus Pocus (1993)

This cult comedy stars Bette Midler, Sarah Jessica Parker and Kathy Najimy as a trio of witches accidentally resurrected in modern‑day Salem. It’s campy, musical and perfect for family viewing. Where to stream: Disney+.

4. Weapons (2024)

A psychological thriller released in 2024, Weapons blends crime and horror with an unsettling narrative. Details remain under wraps to avoid spoilers. Where to stream: Check your preferred rental service for availability.

5. The Conjuring (2013)

Based on true events, this supernatural horror film follows paranormal investigators Ed and Lorraine Warren as they confront an evil presence in a haunted farmhouse. Where to stream: HBO Max or Netflix.

6. Hotel Transylvania (2012)

Dracula runs a hotel for monsters in this animated comedy. When a human stumbles upon the resort, chaos ensues. Ideal for younger viewers. Where to stream: Amazon Prime Video or Hulu.

7. Monster House (2006)

This animated horror film centers on a group of kids who discover that their neighbor’s house is a living, breathing monster. It balances scares with heart. Where to stream: Netflix or Tubi.

8. The Addams Family (1991)

This dark comedy brings the gothic, eccentric Addams clan to life. The film is quirky, macabre and endlessly quotable. Where to stream: Paramount+ or Amazon Prime Video.

9. Casper (1995)

A gentle ghost befriends a teenage girl in this family‑friendly movie that mixes heartwarming moments with light scares. Where to stream: Peacock or Apple TV.

10. Scream (1996)

Wes Craven’s self‑aware slasher reinvigorated horror in the 1990s. A masked killer targets high school students, and the rules of horror movies are turned on their head. Where to stream: Paramount+ or Showtime.

Whether you’re in the mood for family‑friendly fun or bone‑chilling terror, this list covers all bases. Gather your favorite snacks and settle in for a spooky movie marathon.

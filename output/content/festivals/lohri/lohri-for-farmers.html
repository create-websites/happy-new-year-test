Agrarian Rhythm

Gratitude to the Land

Lohri is not just a festival; it is a prayer for survival and hope, symbolizing the transition from winter to spring and honouring the strength of farmers and the potential of the upcoming harvest.

Winter Solstice Passing

As the sun begins its ascent northward, daylight grows longer, boosting crop growth.

Deep Roots in Agriculture

In rural Punjab, farming is more than just a job - it is a lifestyle. Lohri comes during the height of winter, when the wheat is flourishing, serving as a thanksgiving to nature for sustenance.

Rabi Cycle

Related to winter crops such as wheat and mustard, which are typically planted in October and November.

Turning Point

Signaling the conclusion of peak winter and the diminishing risk of early frost.

Community

Farmers come together to commemorate their strength following a season of protecting their crops through the harsh winter nights.

The Farmer's Calendar

Celebrating the Rabi Cycle

Wheat (Kanak)

By the middle of January, the wheat plants are flourishing and well-established. The festival mirrors this growth. "cautious optimism"Grateful for nature's protection as the most delicate stage of growth has passed, despite knowing that the harvest is still months away (April).

Sown: Oct-Nov Harvest: April

Why Jan 13th Matters

  • Frost Danger Passed: The risk of early frost harming young crops has significantly decreased.
  • More Sunlight: Longer days mean stronger photosynthesis for crop development.
  • Winter Rains: Hoping for favorable weather and protection from untimely storms.

Nature & Spirituality

From Fields to Fire

Fire as a Bridge

The bonfire acts as a bridge between field and familyThe warmth of the fire reflects the Sun's growing power, crucial for the growth of crops. Farmers demonstrate their respect for nature by offering peanuts, sesame, and jaggery to the flames.

Intergenerational Wisdom

Lohri fosters connections across generations. Seniors impart their farming wisdom, as youngsters grasp the importance of hard work, dedication, and persistence.

During a time of urban growth, Lohri celebrates the deep connection between people and the land, recognizing agriculture as more than just a means of income, but as a fundamental part of life.
